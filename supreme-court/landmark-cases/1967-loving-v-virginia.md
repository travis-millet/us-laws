# Loving v. Virginia

**Citation:** 388 U.S. 1
**Year Decided:** 1967
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Equal Protection; Due Process)

## Ruling

Laws prohibiting interracial marriage violate the Equal Protection and Due Process Clauses of the 14th Amendment.

## Constitutional Significance

Struck down miscegenation laws in 16 states. Richard and Mildred Loving's case became a touchstone for marriage equality — the name 'Loving' has obvious resonance. Chief Justice Warren's opinion held that marriage is 'one of the basic civil rights of man.' Obergefell v. Hodges (2015) explicitly relied on Loving in extending marriage rights to same-sex couples.
