# Ex parte Milligan

**Citation:** 71 U.S. (4 Wall.) 2
**Year Decided:** 1866
**Constitutional Provisions:** Article III; Amendment 5 (due process); Amendment 6 (jury trial)

## Ruling

Military tribunals cannot try civilians when civilian courts are open and functioning, even during wartime.

## Constitutional Significance

Established limits on military justice and the power of martial law. Reaffirmed that constitutional rights don't evaporate during wartime — a principle tested repeatedly in subsequent wars.
