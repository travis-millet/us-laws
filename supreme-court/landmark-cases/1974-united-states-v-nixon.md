# United States v. Nixon

**Citation:** 418 U.S. 683
**Year Decided:** 1974
**Constitutional Provisions:** Article II (Executive Power); separation of powers

## Ruling

Presidents do not have absolute executive privilege; the President must comply with judicial subpoenas for evidence in criminal proceedings.

## Constitutional Significance

The unanimous decision that ended Richard Nixon's presidency. Nixon had claimed executive privilege to withhold White House tape recordings from the Watergate special prosecutor. The Court held that executive privilege has limits and must yield to the criminal justice system. Nixon resigned 16 days after the ruling. One of the Supreme Court's defining moments in checking executive power.
