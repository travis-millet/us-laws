# Schenck v. United States

**Citation:** 249 U.S. 47
**Year Decided:** 1919
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 1 (Free Speech)

## Ruling

Free speech can be restricted when the words 'create a clear and present danger' of harm; distributing anti-draft leaflets during WWI was not protected.

## Constitutional Significance

Created the 'clear and present danger' test for First Amendment speech restrictions. The 'shouting fire in a crowded theater' analogy (from Justice Holmes's opinion) is among the most famous phrases in constitutional law. The test was later significantly narrowed by Brandenburg v. Ohio (1969).
