# Slaughterhouse Cases

**Citation:** 83 U.S. (16 Wall.) 36
**Year Decided:** 1873
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Privileges or Immunities Clause)

## Ruling

The 14th Amendment's Privileges or Immunities Clause protects only rights of national citizenship, not rights against state interference.

## Constitutional Significance

Severely narrowed the Privileges or Immunities Clause of the 14th Amendment almost immediately after ratification, preventing it from being used to protect civil rights against state action. Forced subsequent civil rights development onto the Due Process and Equal Protection clauses instead.
