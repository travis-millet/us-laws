# Plessy v. Ferguson

**Citation:** 163 U.S. 537
**Year Decided:** 1896
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Equal Protection Clause)

## Ruling

Racial segregation ('separate but equal') does not violate the Equal Protection Clause of the 14th Amendment.

## Constitutional Significance

Provided constitutional cover for Jim Crow laws for 58 years. The 'separate but equal' doctrine was a legal fiction; facilities were never equal. Overruled by Brown v. Board of Education (1954). Justice Harlan's lone dissent — calling the Constitution 'color-blind' — became the foundation for the Brown ruling.
