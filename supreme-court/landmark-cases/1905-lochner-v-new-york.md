# Lochner v. New York

**Citation:** 198 U.S. 45
**Year Decided:** 1905
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Due Process Clause — substantive due process)

## Ruling

A state law limiting bakery workers' hours violated the 'liberty of contract' protected by the 14th Amendment's Due Process Clause.

## Constitutional Significance

The central case of the 'Lochner era' (1905-1937), during which the Court invalidated dozens of economic regulations as violations of 'liberty of contract.' The 'Lochner era' ended with West Coast Hotel v. Parrish (1937) and is now considered a cautionary tale about judicial overreach into economic policy.
