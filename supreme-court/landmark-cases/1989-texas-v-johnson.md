# Texas v. Johnson

**Citation:** 491 U.S. 397
**Year Decided:** 1989
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 1 (Free Speech — symbolic speech)

## Ruling

Burning the American flag as political protest is constitutionally protected speech under the First Amendment.

## Constitutional Significance

Among the most politically controversial decisions in recent decades. Congress immediately passed the Flag Protection Act; the Court struck that down too in United States v. Eichman (1990). Justice Brennan's majority held that the government cannot prohibit expression simply because society finds it disagreeable. Demonstrates First Amendment protection at its most absolute.
