# Gideon v. Wainwright

**Citation:** 372 U.S. 335
**Year Decided:** 1963
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 6 (Right to Counsel); Amendment 14 (incorporation)

## Ruling

Criminal defendants who cannot afford an attorney have a constitutional right to have one provided by the government.

## Constitutional Significance

Landmark right-to-counsel case brought by Clarence Earl Gideon, a Florida prisoner who handwrote his own Supreme Court petition. Created the public defender system as we know it. The story was told in Anthony Lewis's book 'Gideon's Trumpet.' One of the Warren Court's clearest expansions of individual rights.
