# Tinker v. Des Moines Independent Community School District

**Citation:** 393 U.S. 503
**Year Decided:** 1969
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 1 (Free Speech)

## Ruling

Students do not 'shed their constitutional rights at the schoolhouse gate'; public schools cannot prohibit peaceful political expression.

## Constitutional Significance

Arose from students wearing black armbands to protest the Vietnam War. Established that public school students have First Amendment rights, though later decisions (Hazelwood, Morse v. Frederick) have narrowed the scope. 'It can hardly be argued that either students or teachers shed their constitutional rights at the schoolhouse gate' is among the Court's most quoted phrases.
