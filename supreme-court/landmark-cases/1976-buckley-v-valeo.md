# Buckley v. Valeo

**Citation:** 424 U.S. 1
**Year Decided:** 1976
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 1 (Free Speech); campaign finance

## Ruling

Limits on campaign spending are unconstitutional restrictions on free speech; limits on campaign contributions are permissible.

## Constitutional Significance

Established that money in politics = speech for First Amendment purposes. Created the fundamental tension in campaign finance law between anti-corruption interests and free speech that has defined the field ever since. Preceded Citizens United (2010) in establishing the money-as-speech framework.
