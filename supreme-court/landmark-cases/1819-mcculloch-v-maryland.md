# McCulloch v. Maryland

**Citation:** 17 U.S. (4 Wheat.) 316
**Year Decided:** 1819
**Constitutional Provisions:** Article I Section 8 (Necessary and Proper Clause); Article VI (Supremacy Clause)

## Ruling

Congress has implied powers beyond those explicitly listed in the Constitution; states cannot tax federal institutions.

## Constitutional Significance

Established the 'necessary and proper' clause as a broad grant of Congressional power, and the doctrine of federal supremacy over state action. Enabled the expansion of federal power throughout American history.
