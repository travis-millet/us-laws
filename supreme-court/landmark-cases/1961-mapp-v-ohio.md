# Mapp v. Ohio

**Citation:** 367 U.S. 643
**Year Decided:** 1961
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 4 (Search and Seizure); Amendment 14 (incorporation)

## Ruling

Evidence obtained through unconstitutional searches and seizures cannot be used in state criminal prosecutions.

## Constitutional Significance

Incorporated the exclusionary rule (previously only applicable to federal courts) against the states. One of the Warren Court's most significant criminal procedure decisions. Created major practical constraints on law enforcement and generated significant controversy about 'guilty people going free on technicalities.'
