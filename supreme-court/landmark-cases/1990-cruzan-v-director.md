# Cruzan v. Director, Missouri Dept. of Health

**Citation:** 497 U.S. 261
**Year Decided:** 1990
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Due Process — liberty interest)

## Ruling

Competent individuals have a constitutional right to refuse medical treatment; states may require clear evidence of such wishes before allowing withdrawal of life support.

## Constitutional Significance

The Court's first right-to-die case, involving Nancy Cruzan who had been in a persistent vegetative state after a car accident. Established the constitutional framework for end-of-life decisions and informed consent. Led directly to the legal and ethical framework for advance directives and living wills.
