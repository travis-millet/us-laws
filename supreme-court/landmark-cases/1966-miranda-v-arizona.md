# Miranda v. Arizona

**Citation:** 384 U.S. 436
**Year Decided:** 1966
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 5 (Self-Incrimination); Amendment 6 (Right to Counsel)

## Ruling

Police must inform suspects of their constitutional rights — to remain silent, to have an attorney — before custodial interrogation.

## Constitutional Significance

Created the Miranda warning, now embedded in American popular culture through countless TV shows and movies. Generated enormous controversy about whether it hampered law enforcement. The Warren Court's most contested criminal procedure decision. Congress passed a law trying to overturn it; the Supreme Court reaffirmed Miranda in Dickerson v. United States (2000).
