# Watkins v. United States

**Citation:** 354 U.S. 178
**Year Decided:** 1957
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 1 (implied); Amendment 5 (due process)

## Ruling

Congressional investigative power has limits; witnesses cannot be compelled to answer questions about matters not pertinent to a valid legislative purpose.

## Constitutional Significance

Established limits on congressional investigations during the McCarthy era, protecting witnesses from unlimited congressional inquiry. A check on legislative power during one of the most fraught periods in American political history.
