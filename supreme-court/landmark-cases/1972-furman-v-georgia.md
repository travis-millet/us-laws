# Furman v. Georgia

**Citation:** 408 U.S. 238
**Year Decided:** 1972
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 8 (Cruel and Unusual Punishment)

## Ruling

The death penalty as then administered was cruel and unusual punishment; all existing death sentences were commuted.

## Constitutional Significance

Effectively imposed a moratorium on capital punishment in the United States. Nine justices wrote nine separate opinions — no majority rationale. States responded by rewriting death penalty statutes; the moratorium ended in Gregg v. Georgia (1976). Furman and Gregg together dramatically shaped the modern death penalty system.
