# Bostock v. Clayton County

**Citation:** 590 U.S. 644
**Year Decided:** 2020
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Equal Protection — incorporated via statutory interpretation of Title VII)

## Ruling

Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964's prohibition on sex discrimination covers discrimination based on sexual orientation and gender identity.

## Constitutional Significance

Justice Gorsuch — Trump's first Supreme Court nominee — wrote the majority opinion protecting LGBTQ workers from employment discrimination. Held that discriminating against someone for being gay or transgender necessarily involves discrimination 'because of sex.' Justice Alito's dissent accused the majority of legislating from the bench. The textual analysis is a demonstration of Justice Gorsuch's textualist methodology applied to a progressive outcome.
