# Brown v. Board of Education

**Citation:** 347 U.S. 483
**Year Decided:** 1954
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Equal Protection Clause)

## Ruling

Segregated public schools are inherently unequal and violate the Equal Protection Clause of the 14th Amendment.

## Constitutional Significance

The most important Supreme Court decision of the 20th century. Overruled Plessy v. Ferguson (1896). Chief Justice Warren's unanimous opinion held that 'separate educational facilities are inherently unequal.' Became the legal foundation for the entire civil rights movement. The decision's implementation — 'with all deliberate speed' — was contested for decades.
