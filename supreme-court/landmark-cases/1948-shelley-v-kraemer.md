# Shelley v. Kraemer

**Citation:** 334 U.S. 1
**Year Decided:** 1948
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Equal Protection; state action doctrine)

## Ruling

Courts cannot enforce racially restrictive covenants in real estate — judicial enforcement of private discrimination constitutes state action.

## Constitutional Significance

Expanded the 'state action' doctrine to hold that even purely private agreements become state action when courts enforce them. Made racially restrictive covenants legally unenforceable, though they remained on paper in many deeds until the Fair Housing Act of 1968.
