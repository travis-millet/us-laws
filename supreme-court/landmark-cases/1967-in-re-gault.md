# In re Gault

**Citation:** 387 U.S. 1
**Year Decided:** 1967
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Due Process); Amendment 5; Amendment 6

## Ruling

Minors accused of delinquency have constitutional rights to notice, counsel, the privilege against self-incrimination, and the right to confront witnesses.

## Constitutional Significance

Extended core due process rights to juvenile proceedings. Before Gault, juveniles could be committed to reform schools without any of the procedural protections adults had in criminal proceedings. Justice Fortas: 'Under our Constitution, the condition of being a boy does not justify a kangaroo court.'
