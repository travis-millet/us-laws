# Wickard v. Filburn

**Citation:** 317 U.S. 111
**Year Decided:** 1942
**Constitutional Provisions:** Article I Section 8 (Commerce Clause)

## Ruling

Congress can regulate even purely local and personal economic activity if it has a substantial aggregate effect on interstate commerce.

## Constitutional Significance

Roscoe Filburn grew wheat on his own farm for his own use. The Court held this still fell under the Commerce Clause because of aggregate effects. Established the broadest possible interpretation of the Commerce Clause, enabling vast federal regulatory power from the New Deal through the modern era. Not limited until United States v. Lopez (1995).
