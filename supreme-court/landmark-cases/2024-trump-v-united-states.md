# Trump v. United States

**Citation:** 603 U.S. 593
**Year Decided:** 2024
**Constitutional Provisions:** Article II (Executive Power); separation of powers

## Ruling

Former presidents have absolute immunity from criminal prosecution for official acts taken while in office; the scope of immunity for unofficial acts must be determined by lower courts.

## Constitutional Significance

The most consequential separation-of-powers decision in decades. Chief Justice Roberts's majority created a new immunity doctrine for former presidents that the Constitution's text doesn't explicitly establish. Justice Sotomayor's dissent: 'The President is now a king above the law.' Delayed the federal criminal prosecution of former President Trump. The long-term implications for presidential accountability are deeply contested.
