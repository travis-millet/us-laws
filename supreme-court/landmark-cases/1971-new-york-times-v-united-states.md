# New York Times Co. v. United States

**Citation:** 403 U.S. 713
**Year Decided:** 1971
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 1 (Freedom of the Press — prior restraint)

## Ruling

The government cannot exercise prior restraint to prevent publication of the Pentagon Papers.

## Constitutional Significance

The 'Pentagon Papers' case. The Nixon administration sought to prevent the New York Times and Washington Post from publishing classified documents about the Vietnam War. The Court rejected prior restraint — government censorship before publication — as incompatible with the First Amendment. The decision enabled reporting that fundamentally shaped public understanding of the Vietnam War.
