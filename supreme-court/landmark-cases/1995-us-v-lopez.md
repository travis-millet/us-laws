# United States v. Lopez

**Citation:** 514 U.S. 549
**Year Decided:** 1995
**Constitutional Provisions:** Article I Section 8 (Commerce Clause); federalism

## Ruling

Congress cannot use the Commerce Clause to criminalize possession of firearms near schools; the activity must substantially affect interstate commerce.

## Constitutional Significance

First case in 60 years to strike down a federal law as exceeding the Commerce Clause. Ended the post-New Deal assumption that Congress's commerce power was essentially unlimited. Signaled the Rehnquist Court's willingness to enforce constitutional limits on federal power — federalism as a real constraint.
