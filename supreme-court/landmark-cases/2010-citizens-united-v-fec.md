# Citizens United v. Federal Election Commission

**Citation:** 558 U.S. 310
**Year Decided:** 2010
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 1 (Free Speech — corporate speech)

## Ruling

Corporations and unions have First Amendment rights to spend unlimited money on political speech; the government cannot restrict independent political expenditures.

## Constitutional Significance

Transformed American campaign finance. Justice Kennedy's majority held that corporate political speech is protected under the First Amendment. Enabled the rise of Super PACs and dark money in politics. President Obama criticized the ruling during a State of the Union address with Justices sitting in the front row — Justice Alito visibly mouthed 'not true.' Among the most consequential and contested decisions of the Roberts Court.
