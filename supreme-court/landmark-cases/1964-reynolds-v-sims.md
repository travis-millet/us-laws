# Reynolds v. Sims

**Citation:** 377 U.S. 533
**Year Decided:** 1964
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Equal Protection — equal voting weight)

## Ruling

Both houses of state legislatures must be apportioned based on population ('one person, one vote').

## Constitutional Significance

Required state legislative districts to have roughly equal populations. Chief Justice Warren called this his most important decision (more than Brown). Effectively shifted political power from rural areas to cities and suburbs across the country. Congress tried to pass an amendment overturning it; it failed.
