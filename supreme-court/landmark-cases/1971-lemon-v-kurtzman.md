# Lemon v. Kurtzman

**Citation:** 403 U.S. 602
**Year Decided:** 1971
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 1 (Establishment Clause)

## Ruling

Government programs must have a secular purpose, must not primarily advance or inhibit religion, and must avoid excessive government entanglement with religion.

## Constitutional Significance

Created the 'Lemon test' for Establishment Clause cases — a three-part framework for evaluating government's relationship with religion. Used for decades but widely criticized as unpredictable. The Roberts Court has moved away from the Lemon test, replacing it with a historical practice analysis (Kennedy v. Bremerton School District, 2022).
