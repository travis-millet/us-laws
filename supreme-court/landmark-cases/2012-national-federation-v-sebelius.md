# National Federation of Independent Business v. Sebelius

**Citation:** 567 U.S. 519
**Year Decided:** 2012
**Constitutional Provisions:** Article I Section 8 (Commerce Clause; Taxing Power); spending power

## Ruling

The Affordable Care Act's individual mandate is constitutional as a tax; the Medicaid expansion's coercive penalty on states is unconstitutional.

## Constitutional Significance

The Obamacare case. Chief Justice Roberts joined the liberal bloc to uphold the ACA's mandate — but on a rationale (the taxing power) that the government had barely argued. The Medicaid holding (that the federal government couldn't effectively force states to expand Medicaid) was the Court's first significant limit on the spending power. Roberts's vote is still debated — did he follow the law or protect the Court's institutional credibility?
