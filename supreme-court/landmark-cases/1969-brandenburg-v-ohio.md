# Brandenburg v. Ohio

**Citation:** 395 U.S. 444
**Year Decided:** 1969
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 1 (Free Speech)

## Ruling

Government cannot punish inflammatory speech unless it is directed to inciting imminent lawless action and is likely to produce such action.

## Constitutional Significance

The modern First Amendment free speech standard, replacing the weaker 'clear and present danger' test from Schenck v. United States. Established a high bar for speech restrictions that explains why American free speech law is among the most protective in the world. A Ku Klux Klan leader's speech was at issue — the Court protected even hateful speech unless tied to imminent violence.
