# Civil Rights Cases

**Citation:** 109 U.S. 3
**Year Decided:** 1883
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendments 13, 14

## Ruling

Congress cannot prohibit private racial discrimination under the 13th and 14th Amendments; those amendments only restrict government action, not private conduct.

## Constitutional Significance

Struck down the Civil Rights Act of 1875, which had prohibited racial discrimination in public accommodations. Established the 'state action doctrine' — the principle that constitutional rights only protect against government action, not private discrimination. The legal gap this created wasn't filled until the Civil Rights Act of 1964.
