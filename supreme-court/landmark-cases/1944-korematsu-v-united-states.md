# Korematsu v. United States

**Citation:** 323 U.S. 214
**Year Decided:** 1944
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Equal Protection Clause — badly misapplied); strict scrutiny (established here but then ignored)

## Ruling

Japanese American internment during WWII was constitutional as a wartime military necessity.

## Constitutional Significance

Another of the Court's most condemned decisions. Justice Jackson's dissent warned the decision 'lies about like a loaded weapon' for future government overreach. Formally disavowed by the Supreme Court in Trump v. Hawaii (2018). The internment of 120,000 Japanese Americans is now understood as a product of racism, not military necessity.
