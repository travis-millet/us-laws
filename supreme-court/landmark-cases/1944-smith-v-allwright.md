# Smith v. Allwright

**Citation:** 321 U.S. 649
**Year Decided:** 1944
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 15; Amendment 14 (Equal Protection)

## Ruling

Primary elections must be open to voters of all races; the 'white primary' system used in Southern states is unconstitutional.

## Constitutional Significance

Struck down the Democratic Party's all-white primary in Texas, which had effectively disenfranchised Black voters since the party controlled all meaningful political decisions in the one-party South. An important but ultimately incomplete step — Jim Crow disenfranchisement continued through other means until the Voting Rights Act.
