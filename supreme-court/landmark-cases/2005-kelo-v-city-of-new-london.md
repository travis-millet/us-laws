# Kelo v. City of New London

**Citation:** 545 U.S. 469
**Year Decided:** 2005
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 5 (Takings Clause — 'public use')

## Ruling

Government can use eminent domain to take private property and transfer it to a private developer for economic development purposes.

## Constitutional Significance

One of the most unpopular Supreme Court decisions in recent memory, generating rare bipartisan backlash. The Court held that 'public use' under the 5th Amendment includes economic development. 45 states subsequently passed laws restricting economic development takings. Suzette Kelo's house was ultimately demolished; the development project was never built.
