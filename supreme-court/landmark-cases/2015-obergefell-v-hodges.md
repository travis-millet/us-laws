# Obergefell v. Hodges

**Citation:** 576 U.S. 644
**Year Decided:** 2015
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Due Process — fundamental right to marry; Equal Protection)

## Ruling

States must license and recognize marriages between same-sex couples under the Due Process and Equal Protection Clauses of the 14th Amendment.

## Constitutional Significance

The same-sex marriage decision. Justice Kennedy's majority opinion, joined by four liberal justices, held that marriage is a fundamental right that cannot be denied to same-sex couples. The opinion's closing passage, about petitioner Jim Obergefell seeking to have his name on his deceased husband's death certificate, is among the most emotional in Supreme Court history. Chief Justice Roberts's dissent opened: 'Just who do we think we are?'
