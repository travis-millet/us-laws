# Dred Scott v. Sandford

**Citation:** 60 U.S. (19 How.) 393
**Year Decided:** 1857
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 5 (misapplied); overruled by Amendments 13, 14

## Ruling

Enslaved people and their descendants could not be U.S. citizens and had no right to sue in federal court; Congress could not prohibit slavery in the territories.

## Constitutional Significance

Widely considered the Supreme Court's worst decision. It accelerated the Civil War and was directly overruled by the 13th and 14th Amendments. A case study in judicial catastrophe — the Court trying to resolve a political crisis and making it dramatically worse.
