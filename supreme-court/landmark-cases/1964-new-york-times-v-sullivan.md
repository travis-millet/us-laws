# New York Times Co. v. Sullivan

**Citation:** 376 U.S. 254
**Year Decided:** 1964
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 1 (Freedom of the Press)

## Ruling

Public officials cannot win defamation suits unless they prove the defendant acted with 'actual malice' — knowing the statement was false or acting with reckless disregard for truth.

## Constitutional Significance

The cornerstone of American defamation law and press freedom. Set an extremely high bar for public officials to win libel suits, enabling vigorous press coverage of government. The 'actual malice' standard distinguishes US defamation law from most other countries' and is the reason American media can report aggressively on public figures.
