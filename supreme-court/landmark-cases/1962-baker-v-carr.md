# Baker v. Carr

**Citation:** 369 U.S. 186
**Year Decided:** 1962
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Equal Protection); Article III (justiciability)

## Ruling

Federal courts have jurisdiction over legislative apportionment (redistricting) challenges; the political question doctrine does not bar such review.

## Constitutional Significance

Opened federal courts to redistricting challenges, leading directly to Reynolds v. Sims (1964) and the 'one person, one vote' rule. Before Baker, rural-dominated state legislatures refused to redraw districts as populations shifted to cities, giving rural voters vastly disproportionate power. Justice Frankfurter's dissent warned the Court was entering a 'political thicket.'
