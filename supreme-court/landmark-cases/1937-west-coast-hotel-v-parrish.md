# West Coast Hotel Co. v. Parrish

**Citation:** 300 U.S. 379
**Year Decided:** 1937
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Due Process Clause)

## Ruling

Minimum wage laws for women are constitutional; overturning the 'liberty of contract' doctrine of the Lochner era.

## Constitutional Significance

Ended the Lochner era, in which the Court had repeatedly struck down economic regulations. Known as 'the switch in time that saved nine' — Justice Owen Roberts's change of position came as FDR was threatening to pack the Court with additional justices. Changed the Court's approach to economic regulation fundamentally.
