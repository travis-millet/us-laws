# South Carolina v. Katzenbach

**Citation:** 383 U.S. 301
**Year Decided:** 1966
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 15; Amendment 14

## Ruling

The Voting Rights Act of 1965 is constitutional under Congress's power to enforce the 15th Amendment.

## Constitutional Significance

Upheld the VRA's preclearance requirement, which required covered jurisdictions to seek federal approval before changing voting rules. Established that Congress has broad power to enforce voting rights. The contrast with Shelby County v. Holder (2013) is stark — the same preclearance provision upheld in 1966 was struck down as outdated in 2013.
