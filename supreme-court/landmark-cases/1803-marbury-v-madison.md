# Marbury v. Madison

**Citation:** 5 U.S. (1 Cranch) 137
**Year Decided:** 1803
**Constitutional Provisions:** Article III; Article VI (Supremacy Clause)

## Ruling

The Supreme Court has the power to strike down laws that violate the Constitution (judicial review).

## Constitutional Significance

Established judicial review — the foundation of the entire Supreme Court's power to invalidate legislation and executive action. Chief Justice John Marshall's most enduring legacy. Without this case, the Supreme Court would be a much weaker institution.
