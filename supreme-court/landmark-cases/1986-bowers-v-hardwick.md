# Bowers v. Hardwick

**Citation:** 478 U.S. 186
**Year Decided:** 1986
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Due Process); privacy

## Ruling

States can criminalize consensual same-sex sexual conduct; there is no constitutional right to homosexual sodomy.

## Constitutional Significance

Deeply controversial ruling upholding Georgia's sodomy statute as applied to gay men. Directly overruled 17 years later in Lawrence v. Texas (2003), with Justice Kennedy calling the earlier decision 'not correct when it was decided.' One of the clearest examples of the Court reversing itself on a major rights question within living memory.
