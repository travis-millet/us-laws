# Shelby County v. Holder

**Citation:** 570 U.S. 529
**Year Decided:** 2013
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 15 (Voting Rights); Amendment 14; VRA Section 4(b)

## Ruling

The Voting Rights Act's coverage formula for determining which jurisdictions must seek federal preclearance is unconstitutional as outdated.

## Constitutional Significance

Effectively gutted the Voting Rights Act of 1965's preclearance requirement, which had required certain (mostly Southern) jurisdictions to get federal approval before changing voting rules. Chief Justice Roberts held the formula was based on 40-year-old data. Justice Ginsburg's dissent compared it to 'throwing away your umbrella in a rainstorm because you are not getting wet.' Within hours of the decision, states began implementing voting restrictions that had previously been blocked.
