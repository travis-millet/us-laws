# Regents of the University of California v. Bakke

**Citation:** 438 U.S. 265
**Year Decided:** 1978
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Equal Protection Clause)

## Ruling

Racial quotas in university admissions are unconstitutional, but race can be considered as one factor in a holistic admissions process.

## Constitutional Significance

The foundational affirmative action case. Justice Powell's controlling opinion introduced 'diversity' as a compelling government interest justifying race-conscious admissions. The Bakke framework governed university admissions for 45 years until Students for Fair Admissions v. Harvard (2023).
