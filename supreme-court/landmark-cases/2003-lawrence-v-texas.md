# Lawrence v. Texas

**Citation:** 539 U.S. 558
**Year Decided:** 2003
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Due Process — liberty and dignity)

## Ruling

Laws criminalizing consensual same-sex sexual conduct violate the Due Process Clause; Bowers v. Hardwick is overruled.

## Constitutional Significance

Justice Kennedy's opinion dismantled Bowers v. Hardwick and established that intimate personal relationships between adults are protected by the Due Process Clause. The opinion's language about dignity and autonomy laid the groundwork for Obergefell. Justice Scalia's furious dissent predicted it would lead to same-sex marriage — he was right.
