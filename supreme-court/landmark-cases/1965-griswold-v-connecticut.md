# Griswold v. Connecticut

**Citation:** 381 U.S. 479
**Year Decided:** 1965
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Due Process — substantive due process); Amendments 1, 3, 4, 5, 9 (privacy penumbras)

## Ruling

Laws criminalizing the use of contraception by married couples violate the constitutional right to privacy.

## Constitutional Significance

Created the 'right to privacy' as a constitutional doctrine, found in the 'penumbras' of the Bill of Rights. Became the foundation for Roe v. Wade and a series of cases protecting intimate personal decisions from government interference. Justice Douglas's opinion finding privacy in 'penumbras formed by emanations' is among the most debated in constitutional law.
