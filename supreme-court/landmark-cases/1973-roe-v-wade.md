# Roe v. Wade

**Citation:** 410 U.S. 113
**Year Decided:** 1973
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Due Process — substantive due process/privacy)

## Ruling

Women have a constitutional right to abortion before fetal viability; this right is part of the constitutional right to privacy.

## Constitutional Significance

One of the most controversial decisions in Court history, generating 49 years of political conflict before being overruled by Dobbs v. Jackson Women's Health Organization (2022). Created a trimester framework for abortion regulation. Justice Blackmun's opinion was immediately attacked as legislating from the bench. The political coalition it created — uniting evangelical Christians and Catholics against abortion — reshaped American politics.
