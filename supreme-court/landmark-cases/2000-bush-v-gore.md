# Bush v. Gore

**Citation:** 531 U.S. 98
**Year Decided:** 2000
**Constitutional Provisions:** Amendment 14 (Equal Protection — equal administration of elections); Article II

## Ruling

The Florida Supreme Court's recount procedures were unconstitutional; the recount could not be completed under valid standards before the federal deadline.

## Constitutional Significance

The decision that ended the 2000 presidential election recount and made George W. Bush president. Deeply controversial — five conservative justices halted the recount; the per curiam opinion notably stated it was 'limited to present circumstances.' The Court's legitimacy took significant damage. Justice Stevens's dissent: 'Although we may never know with complete certainty the identity of the winner of this year's Presidential election, the identity of the loser is perfectly clear. It is the Nation's confidence in the judge as an impartial guardian of the rule of law.'
